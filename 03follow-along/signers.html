Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Here are a few influential figures among the 56 signers of the Declaration of Independence:

Benjamin Franklin
As the oldest signer at 70, Franklin was a polymath involved in science, printing, and diplomacy. He was instrumental in gaining French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
He played a critical role in advocating for independence and later became the first Vice President and second President of the United States.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration, Jefferson was the third President of the USA and a key figure in the initial formative years of the nation.
John Hancock
Known for his prominent and stylish signature, Hancock was the President of the Second Continental Congress and a significant political leader of his time.
Samuel Adams
A key organizer of resistance against British rule, Samuel Adams was a fervent advocate of colonial independence.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer, Carroll was a wealthy plantation owner and influential political leader in Maryland.
Roger Sherman
The only person who signed all four major Revolutionary documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.

Their contributions were instrumental in shaping the United States and guiding it through its early stages.
